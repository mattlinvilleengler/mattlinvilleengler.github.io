Emilia Levin Fitness

About

Born and raised in the Bay Area, fitness has always been a part of my life. I began swimming as far back as I can remember and was dedicated to the sport until I left for college. I played basketball and ran cross country in high school. Hiking was always (and still is) a part of my summer adventures.

Leaving for college I was under the firm belief that I would become a lawyer. I graduated with a degree in International Affairs and a certificate in Jewish Studies. Unfortunately, once I graduated I knew this wasn’t the path I was going to take. It wasn’t where my true passion lied. I turned to personal training when I decided I wanted to get into better shape and learn more about myself and my capabilities.

I have always been passionate about nutrition and healthy eating, especially growing up with a mom who makes everything from scratch with the best ingredients out there. The combination of exercise and nutrition made me realize that this is exactly what I wanted to do with my life. I have been a personal trainer for over three years and have been fortunate enough to train in Jackson Hole, Austin, and Boulder.

Coaching

My training philosophy is to help others learn about their bodies, including their physical attributes and their potential for creating new possibilities for themselves. Living a healthy lifestyle is of the utmost importance to me and I hope to share my experience and insight with individuals who are looking to make exercise and fitness a lifetime pursuit and passion.

I believe in setting challenging but achievable goals that you will not only be able to accomplish, but will even exceed. I work with individuals on a personal level, in order to create exercise routines that will fit your specific needs. I take into consideration all factors of your exercise history andmotivation, current or past medical conditions, injuries, and likes and dislikes about working out.

My training methodology combines exercise and healthy eating. I customize diverse exercise regimens that emphasize technique and form. I include various modalities in my instruction such as mobility and stretching, Crossfit, TRX, weight and circuit training. I am personally committed to addressing the specific fitness goals of each individual.

Spending just an hour a day is all it takes to give your body the appreciation it deserves. I will challenge you and I will push you to achieve new levels and realize your potential without injury. You will work hard during our session and I promise they will be varied, energizing, and educational, leaving you with a greater understanding of yourself and your capabilities.

Nutrition

Eating is easy, natural and for the most part, an enjoyable experience. Nutrition, however, is a daunting, confusing, and ever changing subject. Facts and opinions on the impact of diet on health and longevity continue to evolve, often resulting in complete confusion over what is best to eat for any one’s body type and daily needs. Building muscle and reaching new plateaus of fitness is nutrition dependent and it is critical that certain, specific nutrients are consumed before and after exercise periods. Exercise in the absence of nutritional considerations will not allow anyone to achieve their fitness goals.

I will help clarify and decipher diets and fads that are popular today and provide information about how and which foods can impact fitness goals. It is important to understand macronutrients (fats, proteins, carbohydrates) and the role each one of these plays in the exercising body. We will discuss sugar intake (how much is too much?), fruits and vegetables, the importance of antioxidants…and anything else you can think of!

I am passionate about cooking. I experiment with recipes often and I am excited to share ideas about foods you will look forward to eating on a daily and weekly basis. I will help you create weekly meal plans with preparation tips that will make cooking seem less overwhelming and time intensive. I will provide suggestions concerning diet and calorie intake, as well as make recommendations for alternatives to processed, sugary foods that only diminish the maximum potential one can accomplish from each and every workout.
